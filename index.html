    AI Therapist


    A Pearl - 2021


    This piece was inspired by one of my favorite songs, titled the same, by my favorite artist: Mitski. She is a Japenese-American artist and her music often contains themes about the joys and trials of being an Asian American woman. She also poetically incorperates the experience of living with mental illness into a lot of her discography. This song particulary spoke to me because of the emotionally charged instrumental; throughout the song, the listener has a feeling of fighting the waves coming at her, needing to come up for air. Mitski captures the feeling of struggling daily with mental illness, despite trying everyday to heal. However, the last few moments of the song return to a feeling of calmness, what I like to think of as the hope at the end of the tunnel. This painting is my interpretation of these feelings. Despite the mental storms you experience, every day is a new chance for change.

    My Muse


    My Muse - 2018


    This piece, also inspired by music, came from a project I completed in sophomore year of HIGH SCHOOL. Throwback. The task was to depict something that inspires you - I chose concerts. Concerts are the closest thing we have to teleportation, in my opinion. As soon as the artist comes on, you are sent into another dimension, one where the artist paints the lands that you will inhabit for the next 2-3 hours. This particular scene was inspired by a lyric from Lorde's "Hard Feelings/Loveless", a song I got to see live that year. We had to first map out what we were going to paint using magazine clippings, and then had to paint our canvas using that as a guide. It was a technique I really enjoyed because I learned to pay extra attention to the details of each component of the painting, rather than approaching it all at once.

    Une Belle Nuit


    Une Belle Nuit - 2024


    This is one of my favorite paintings ever. Partly because it looked NOTHING like this when I first started it. I was nervous to take on this painting because of how intricately beautiful the Eiffel Tower is, so of course, I went in with no plan. The first iteration of this painting honestly turned out looking like a wonky giraffe. After a break with a fresh set of eyes and some helpful advice, I was able to turn it into this piece of work that I'm really proud of. I was grateful to have been able to visit Paris just a couple month before I painted this and I feel that I captured the magic of love that fills the air there in this painting. It's not perfect, but it's the time, effort, and continued dedication that makes it perfect to me :)

    Bollywood Thru The Ages


    My Bharatanatyam Journey


    This performance was my last one I did at UConn, which is why I decided to include it here. We depicted various decades of Indian music throughout this piece including 8 popular Bollywood songs, 5 different South Asian languages, and 3 beautiful Indian classical art forms. It's also the one I'm most proud of because 1) I got to choreograph Apasara Aali, the closing song 2) I created the mix for it, which was a lot of fun 3) most of all, I got to have so many fun moments learning it with my favorite team in the world, UConn Sanskriti. I joined Sanskriti as a freshman, when there were about 5 people in the club and everyone had to join remotely because of COVID. Along with everyone in that video and others, we built the club into something I'm proud to say will leave a mark on the Indian cultural community at UConn. I was also grateful for the opportunity to continue dancing in college, even after finishing my arangetram (the debut solo dance recital of one's dance journey) a couple years prior. Bharatanatyam is what gets me into my flow state and I'm grateful to have something through which I can continue to connect with my culture, all while having a community of wonderful dancers to learn from.
